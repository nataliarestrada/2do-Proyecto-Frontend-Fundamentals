Roma

Any list of places to see in Italy must necessarily include its capital.

Rome is, without question, one of the most beautiful cities in the world and any trip to Italy should spend at least a few days in it.

It is known as the "Eternal City" for the History that each of its nooks and crannies encloses, but in Rome you can also enjoy cutting-edge attractions such as haute cuisine restaurants and shopping streets that can only be found here.

Venecia

Venice is one of the most romantic cities in the world and one of the places to visit in Italy whatever the time of year.

The city of canals exudes sensuality from every corner, especially in its big festival, the Carnival.

Tower of Pisa

The Tower of Pisa is the emblem of the city of Pisa, Italy and is one of the most loved and recognized monuments in the whole world. The work is located in Piazza del Duomo, in the heart of urban planning. To go it is necessary to approach Tuscany, preferably by train from the city of Rome, about 35 minutes away.

Val d’Orcia, Toscana

The Val d'Orcia, in the Tuscany region of Italy, is the rural heart of the country. Driving the Strada di Valori, a road lined on both sides by regimental cypress trees, from La Foce to Monticchiello is like driving through an agricultural time capsule.
